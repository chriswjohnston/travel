Step 01 · 2044–2048 · Undergraduate

BSc Marine Biology

Dalhousie University · Halifax, Nova Scotia

Dalhousie's Department of Biology offers one of Canada's leading undergraduate Marine Biology programs, with direct access to the Northwest Atlantic Ocean, the Bedford Institute of Oceanography, and world-class research vessels operating in some of the most biologically productive waters on Earth. Halifax is a working port city deeply connected to the ocean — the ideal first step for JJ coming from a farm background into formal marine science.

Why Dalhousie? Located on the Atlantic coast JJ first explored in Newfoundland at age 14. The Maritimes maritime culture, proximity to Iceberg Alley, and the Bedford Institute partnership mean field opportunities begin in Year 1. Dalhousie has one of the strongest marine mammal research groups in Canada — directly aligned with JJ's orca and humpback experiences.

Step 02 · 2048–2050 · Graduate

MSc Earth & Ocean Sciences

University of Victoria · Victoria, British Columbia

The University of Victoria's School of Earth and Ocean Sciences (SEOS) is consistently ranked among Canada's top graduate programs for oceanography, marine geoscience, and climate science. Victoria sits at the junction of the Pacific Ocean, the Strait of Juan de Fuca, and the southern tip of Vancouver Island — the same coastline JJ will have dived at age 18. The proximity to the Institute of Ocean Sciences in Sidney, BC and the Pacific marine research network makes UVic an exceptional bridge between Canadian field science and international ocean research.

Why UVic? After experiencing Vancouver Island's orca ecology, kelp forests, and salmon watersheds on the Alaska graduation trip, UVic gives JJ the chance to do graduate research in those same waters. SEOS faculty work on topics directly aligned with the adventure plan arc: Pacific ocean circulation, marine mammal ecology, tidal estuaries, and climate-driven ecosystem change.

Step 03 · 2050–2055 · Doctoral

PhD Oceanography

Scripps Institution of Oceanography · La Jolla, California · UC San Diego

The Scripps Institution of Oceanography at UC San Diego is one of the oldest, largest, and most consequential oceanographic institutions in the world. Founded in 1903, Scripps employs over 300 researchers, operates four major research vessels, and has produced foundational discoveries in ocean circulation, climate science, marine biology, and deep-sea ecology. A PhD from Scripps is a credential recognized at the highest levels of international ocean science. La Jolla sits on the Pacific coast 30 minutes north of San Diego — the same ocean JJ first saw from Cannery Row at age 7.

Why Scripps? Scripps represents the culmination of everything the adventure plan was building toward. The Keeling Curve (measuring atmospheric CO₂) was established at Scripps. The world's understanding of El Niño, deep ocean circulation, and marine biodiversity was significantly advanced by Scripps researchers. A PhD here places JJ at the centre of global ocean science at exactly the moment — mid-21st century — when ocean research is most urgently needed.

After the PhD

Career Pathways

A Scripps PhD opens doors across academia, government science, international conservation, and private research. These are the most aligned pathways given JJ's adventure arc.

Academic Research
University Professor + Principal Investigator
Run an independent research lab at a research university. Canadian options: Dalhousie, UVic, UBC, U of T. International: MBARI, Woods Hole, Plymouth Marine Laboratory. Teach, publish, and lead at-sea expeditions. The path for someone who wants to ask original questions about how the ocean works.
Government Science
Fisheries and Oceans Canada (DFO) Scientist
Canada's Department of Fisheries and Oceans employs hundreds of marine biologists and oceanographers. Research areas: salmon population dynamics, marine mammal health, ocean acidification, Arctic ecosystems. Stable federal career with significant field research opportunities across all three Canadian ocean coasts.
International Conservation
IUCN / Marine Protected Area Science
Work with international bodies (IUCN, UNESCO, UNEP) on marine protected area design, ocean policy, and biodiversity assessment. A Scripps PhD carries significant weight in international policy circles. This path connects ocean science directly to the conservation outcomes JJ encountered at every trip — from manatee rescue at age 3 to orca conservation at age 18.
Deep Ocean Research
MBARI / Deep-Sea Marine Biologist
The Monterey Bay Aquarium Research Institute (MBARI) is one of the world's leading deep-sea research institutions — operating ROVs that explore the ocean floor. Located in Moss Landing, 15 minutes from the Monterey Bay Aquarium JJ first visited at age 7. Full circle: from watching the kelp forest through aquarium glass to operating ROVs in the submarine canyon beneath it.
Climate Science
Ocean-Climate Researcher
The mid-21st century urgency around ocean acidification, warming, deoxygenation, and coral bleaching makes ocean-climate scientists among the most needed researchers in the world. Scripps's Climate, Atmospheric Science and Physical Oceanography division is a global leader. The retreating glaciers JJ saw at age 10 and 18 provide a visceral motivation for this work.
Science Communication
Ocean Educator + Documentarian
A scientist who grew up watching Blue Planet and then became a marine biologist is uniquely placed to communicate ocean science to the public. David Attenborough, Sylvia Earle, and Edith Widder all combined rigorous science with the ability to make it matter to non-scientists. JJ's adventure plan is already a story — there may be a film, book, or educational program in it.

A Note on Costs

The full post-secondary pathway — BSc at Dalhousie, MSc at UVic, PhD at Scripps — carries an estimated total cost of roughly $150,000–200,000 CAD over 13 years for tuition, living costs, and travel. Importantly: PhD programs at institutions like Scripps are typically fully funded with a stipend, meaning JJ would be paid to do his research in the final 4–6 years. NSERC scholarships, provincial awards, and university fellowships can reduce BSc and MSc costs significantly. The adventure plan's total estimated cost of $76,500–103,000 CAD over 15 trips is arguably the best educational investment a family can make before university — experiential learning, scientific curiosity, and ecological literacy built trip by trip.

Purpose

At 10, JJ is entering the age of systems thinking. Banff and Jasper teach geology, glaciology, hydrology, and ecology as a single integrated system — mountains catch water, glaciers store it, rivers distribute it, oceans receive it. This is the trip that connects land and sea at the biggest possible scale.

Farm Connection

JJ understands water on the farm — irrigation, the creek, how dry summers stress the animals. Banff scales that up by a billion. The Columbia Icefield is the water tower of North America — meltwater flows to the Pacific, Atlantic, and Arctic Oceans from the same mountain range. A 10-year-old who knows what a drought means on a farm will understand viscerally why a retreating glacier matters.

Science Questions to Build
  • How do glaciers carve U-shaped valleys vs river-carved V-shapes?
  • What is glacial flour and why does it turn lakes turquoise?
  • Why does the Columbia Icefield feed three oceans simultaneously?
  • How do large animals like wolves need connected habitat corridors?
  • What happens to ecosystems downstream when glaciers retreat?
Trip Goals
  • Understand glaciers as both geological records and water sources
  • See the full chain: mountain snowpack → glacier → river → ocean
  • Observe large predator ecosystems (wolves, bears, cougars)
  • Understand the concept of a watershed as a connected system
  • Draw the complete water cycle from glacier to ocean in the nature journal
Itinerary

8-Day Plan

Day
1
Fly Calgary → Banff Arrival
Calgary → Banff
Fly into Calgary, drive 90 minutes west on Hwy 1. The mountains appear on the horizon while still on the plains — a slow reveal that builds anticipation. Enter Banff National Park at the east gate. Evening walk through Banff townsite. Look for elk on the golf course — they're often right in town. Bow River sunset.
Mountain first revealBow River walkTownsite elk spottingParks Canada pass purchase
Learning focus: mountains appear before you reach them. They control weather, water, and wildlife for hundreds of kilometres around them.
Buy the Parks Canada annual discovery pass at the gate — covers all national parks and pays for itself on this trip.
Day
2
Moraine Lake at Dawn + Lake Louise
Moraine Lake / Lake Louise
Wake at 4:30am for the Parks Canada shuttle to Moraine Lake before crowds. The turquoise colour is glacial flour — rock ground to microscopic particles that scatter blue-green light wavelengths. Rockpile trail (20 min) for the classic elevated view of the Valley of Ten Peaks. Afternoon: Lake Louise shoreline walk and compare the colour to Moraine Lake.
Dawn Moraine Lake shuttleRockpile viewpointValley of Ten PeaksLake Louise shorelineGlacial flour colour explanation
Learning focus: glaciers grind rock into powder. The flour floats in meltwater and scatters blue-green light. The colour is physics, not algae.
Moraine Lake requires advance shuttle booking — private vehicles are banned in peak season. Book Parks Canada shuttle weeks ahead.
Day
3
Johnston Canyon + Banff Gondola
Johnston Canyon / Sulphur Mountain
Johnston Canyon slot canyon walk on catwalks bolted to limestone walls above a rushing creek. Lower Falls (1.1km) and Upper Falls (2.7km) are both excellent for a 10-year-old. The Inkpots — cold mineral springs — are worth the full 6km walk. Afternoon: Banff Gondola to Sulphur Mountain summit (2,281m). Summit boardwalk shows the full layered mountain ecosystem: tree line, alpine meadow, rock summit.
Canyon catwalk hikeLower and Upper FallsInkpots mineral springsGondola summit walkAlpine ecosystem layers
Learning focus: water carves rock over thousands of years. The same process that made Johnston Canyon — the Bow River made the entire valley. Now think about ocean canyons.
Day
4
Icefields Parkway North to Jasper
Banff → Jasper via Icefields Pkwy
The Icefields Parkway is widely regarded as the most spectacular road on Earth. Do not rush it — build in 6+ stops. Key stops: Bow Lake (toe of Crowfoot Glacier), Peyto Lake viewpoint, Mistaya Canyon, Parker Ridge hike (90 min to the edge of the Saskatchewan Glacier), Sunwapta Falls. Each stop teaches one watershed concept. Arrive in Jasper by evening.
Bow Lake + Crowfoot GlacierPeyto Lake viewpointMistaya Canyon hikeParker Ridge hikeSunwapta Falls
Learning focus: one 230km road shows the entire birth-to-river-to-ocean story of glacial meltwater. Every stop is another chapter of the same water system.
Leave Banff by 7am. Parker Ridge is worth the 90-min round trip — the view down onto the Saskatchewan Glacier is one of the most dramatic in the Rockies.
Day
5
Columbia Icefield + Athabasca Glacier
Columbia Icefield
The Columbia Icefield is the largest non-polar icefield in North America — 325 km² of ice up to 365m deep. Walk to the marked glacier edge and stand on 10,000-year-old ice. The marker posts showing glacier retreat since 1890 are among the most sobering climate visuals in the world. Glacier Skywalk — glass-floored platform over a 280m cliff. The meltwater JJ touches today reaches the ocean in roughly 3 months.
Athabasca Glacier ice walkRetreat marker posts (since 1890)Columbia Icefield centreGlacier Skywalk glass platform
Learning focus: this ice feeds three oceans — Pacific, Atlantic, and Arctic — simultaneously. The retreat markers show how much has melted since 1890; in JJ's lifetime it will retreat further than in the prior 100 years.
Day
6
Jasper Wildlife + Maligne Lake
Jasper National Park
Jasper is wilder and less crowded than Banff. Early morning drives along the Yellowhead corridor for elk, coyotes, and occasionally wolves at a distance. Maligne Canyon gorge walk in the morning. Afternoon: Maligne Lake boat tour to Spirit Island — most-photographed location in the Canadian Rockies, surrounded by glaciated peaks. The lake is fed entirely by glacial melt.
Dawn wildlife driveMaligne Canyon gorgeMaligne Lake boat to Spirit IslandGlacier-fed lake ecology
Learning focus: large animals — wolves, bears, elk — need thousands of km² of connected habitat. A wildlife corridor works like a river: cut it off and the whole system collapses.
Day
7
Pyramid Lake + Return South
Jasper → Calgary
Morning: Pyramid Lake and Pyramid Island (footbridge access) for the best mountain reflection view in Jasper. Short hike or canoe rental. Drive south toward Calgary — use the return to revisit favourite Parkway viewpoints. Nature journal final entry: draw the watershed system from glacier to ocean, labelling what JJ saw at each stage. Overnight Calgary or fly home.
Pyramid Island hike or canoeReturn Icefields ParkwayFavourite viewpoint stopsNature journal watershed diagram
Learning focus: can JJ draw the full water cycle — from glacier to river to ocean — using only what he saw this week?

Practical Logistics
  • Moraine Lake shuttle: book via Parks Canada reservation system weeks ahead
  • Maligne Lake boat tour: book at least 2 weeks ahead in July
  • Wildlife viewing windows: 5–8am and dusk are most productive
  • Carry bear spray — available at every outdoor store in Banff and Jasper
  • Glacier walk: waterproof boots and layers — cold on the ice even in July
  • Parker Ridge trail: rated easy-moderate, 2.4km return, well worth it
JJ's Pre-Trip Science Prep

  • Look up the Columbia Icefield on Google Earth — trace the glacier tongues
  • Talk about what a watershed is using the farm's creek as the example
  • Read: Water Dance by Thomas Locker — poetic and scientifically grounded
  • Research one large predator before the trip: wolves, grizzlies, or cougars

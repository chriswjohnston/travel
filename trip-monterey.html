Trip 03 · June 2033 · Age 7 · Grade 1 Completed

Monterey +
Yosemite

Theme: Discovery California Age 7

JJ meets real wild ocean life for the first time — kelp forests, sea otters, tide pools, and humpback whales at Monterey Bay. Then Yosemite teaches the other half: mountains are water machines, and snowmelt feeds every ocean on Earth.

Monterey Bay · California

Yosemite Valley · El Capitan
Purpose

Move from Disney's curated animal experience to real wild ecosystems. Monterey Bay Aquarium is one of the world's finest marine science institutions — JJ will see kelp forests, sea otters, and open ocean animals in their most natural exhibited form. Tide pools at Point Lobos put him in direct contact with living intertidal ecosystems. Then Yosemite teaches that mountains and oceans are connected by water.

Farm Connection

A 7-year-old who lives with horses, goats, and cattle already understands territory, food sources, and protection. The kelp forest framing — "think of it like a forest on the farm, but underwater, and every layer has different animals" — will click immediately. Yosemite's waterfalls tie directly to the watershed logic he sees daily when the farm creek fills and drains.

Itinerary

9-Day Plan

Day
1
Fly SFO → Drive to Monterey
SFO → Monterey
Fly into San Francisco, rent a car, and drive the coastal route to Monterey via Hwy 1. Stop at Half Moon Bay or Moss Landing for the first ocean air and shorebirds. Check in near Cannery Row. Evening walk along the waterfront — sea otters float in the kelp just offshore. This is the first wild ocean encounter, before the aquarium.
Coastal driveHwy 1 first ocean viewsSea otter spotting from shoreCannery Row walk
Learning focus: the ocean is already alive before we enter the aquarium.
Bring binoculars — sea otters float on their backs in the kelp beds right off Monterey Wharf.
Day
2
Monterey Bay Aquarium — Full Day
Monterey Bay Aquarium
The aquarium is world-class — budget a full day. Start in the Kelp Forest exhibit (28-foot living kelp tank with leopard sharks and schooling fish). Sea otter exhibit with rescued otters. Open Sea tank with tuna, sea turtles, and hammerhead sharks. Splash Zone touch pools for sea stars and hermit crabs. 'Science on a Sphere' globe shows ocean temperature and currents in real time.
Kelp Forest exhibitSea otter habitatOpen Sea tankTouch poolsJellyfish exhibitScience on a Sphere
Learning focus: kelp forests are layered habitats — seafloor, mid-water, canopy, surface — each layer supports different species.
Buy tickets online — it sells out. Arrive at opening. Feeding times are announced on arrival and are spectacular.
Day
3
Point Lobos Tide Pools
Point Lobos State Reserve
Plan around low tide (check tide tables the night before — aim for -0.5ft or lower). Walk to Whalers Cove, then Bird Island trail for cormorant and pelican colonies. Tide pools reveal sea anemones, hermit crabs, purple sea urchins, sea stars, shore crabs, chitons, and tide pool fish. Rules: look, don't collect, step only on bare rock. Bring a hand lens — urchins look incredible up close.
Low-tide pool explorationSea star and anemone IDSeabird colony viewingHand lens observationNature journal sketching
Learning focus: tides create windows into hidden ecosystems every 6 hours. The animals in tide pools are as tough as anything on the farm.
Reserve entry in advance — Point Lobos requires day-use reservations and fills fast. Wear shoes with grip for wet rock.
Day
4
Whale Watching + 17-Mile Drive
Monterey Bay offshore
Book a 3–4 hour whale watch. Monterey Bay is one of the top humpback feeding grounds on Earth due to cold upwelling from the submarine canyon beneath the bay. June is peak season. Humpbacks often bubble-net feed — explain that the whale uses air bubbles like a fence to trap thousands of krill, then lunges through with its mouth open. Afternoon: 17-Mile Drive through Pebble Beach — sea otters, pelicans, and dramatic cypress coastline.
Humpback whale watchingDolphin pod encountersBubble-net feeding explanation17-Mile Drive coastal tourLone Cypress viewpoint
Learning focus: the submarine canyon beneath Monterey Bay creates cold upwelling that feeds the entire ecosystem — from krill to whales.
Take Dramamine the night before if anyone is prone to seasickness. Binoculars essential. Ask the naturalist on board to explain what they see.
Day
5
Drive to Yosemite
Monterey → Yosemite Valley
Drive east through the Salinas Valley then climb into the Sierra Nevada. Watch the vegetation change as elevation rises: coastal scrub to oak grassland to ponderosa pine to fir forest. Arrive via Big Oak Flat entrance and stop at Tunnel View — the first full reveal of Yosemite Valley, El Capitan, Half Dome, and Bridalveil Fall. Evening valley floor walk.
Elevation vegetation change observationTunnel View valley revealBridalveil Fall viewValley floor evening walk
Learning focus: mountains collect water from a massive area and concentrate it into rivers. Every waterfall here was snow a few weeks ago.
Day
6
Yosemite Valley — Waterfalls and Wildlife
Yosemite Valley
Lower Yosemite Fall trail — 1 mile round trip, flat, and staggering (tallest waterfall in North America). Cook's Meadow loop for black bear sightings, deer, and El Capitan reflection in the Merced River. Mirror Lake for the Half Dome reflection. Bring lunch and eat by the river — watch for American dippers and Steller's jays.
Lower Yosemite Fall hikeCook's Meadow wildlife walkEl Capitan viewpointMirror Lake shuttleMerced River lunch
Learning focus: June snowmelt becomes the waterfalls. The same water in this river reaches the Pacific Ocean within weeks. Ask JJ: where does this water end up?
Timed-entry permits required — book months ahead at recreation.gov. Valley shuttles are free and frequent.
Day
7
Mariposa Grove — Giant Sequoias
Mariposa Grove
Giant sequoias are the largest living things on Earth by volume — up to 275 feet tall and 3,000 years old. The Grizzly Giant trail (2 miles round trip) is manageable for a 7-year-old. Stand at the base and look up. Let JJ put his arms around the tree to feel the scale. These trees survived because their bark is thick and fire-resistant — a good ecosystem conversation about adaptation.
Grizzly Giant trailCalifornia Tunnel Tree walk-throughForest ecology discussionNature journal — sketch a sequoia
Learning focus: these trees were alive when the Roman Empire was at its height. Scale means something different when it's measured in centuries.
Day
8
Glacier Point + Return Toward SFO
Glacier Point → SFO
Morning: Glacier Point road for a high viewpoint above the valley (Half Dome, Nevada Fall, Vernal Fall all visible at once). Drive west toward San Francisco. Airport hotel or direct evening flight. Final recap conversation: favourite marine animal, favourite landscape, biggest surprise, and one thing JJ wants to learn more about — write it in the nature journal.
Glacier Point viewpointValley overviewSFO driveTrip recap + nature journal
Learning focus: turning experience into memory — what did we observe, what did it mean, what do we want to know next?

Practical Logistics
  • Book Yosemite timed-entry permits the moment they open — recreation.gov, months ahead
  • Point Lobos reservations: book online, limited day-use spots
  • Whale watch: Princess Monterey Whale Watching or Sanctuary Cruises — both excellent
  • Tide tables: check tideschart.com the night before Point Lobos
  • Mariposa Grove shuttle required in peak season — no private vehicles at the grove
  • June snow is gone from the valley but Glacier Point road may open late — check ahead
